About Us

Teenage pregnancy in the Philippines is a complex issue with serious social, economic, and health consequences. Many young girls face increased risks of pregnancy complications, interrupted education, and limited job opportunities, which often trap them in poverty. Their children are also more likely to experience health and developmental challenges, continuing the cycle of disadvantage. This problem places additional strain on healthcare systems and social services, affecting the country's overall development. Addressing teenage pregnancy requires comprehensive sex education, accessible reproductive health services, and community support to empower young people with knowledge and opportunities for a better future. Without intervention, the long-term impacts will continue to hinder progress and equality in society. Breaking this cycle is essential for building healthier, more prosperous communities.
