Consequences



Financial Consequences


• Reduced Educational Attainment: Teenage mothers are more likely to drop out of school.

• Increased Reliance on Public Assistance: Teenage mothers often require financial support from government programs.

• Economic Hardship: Raising a child is expensive, and teenage mothers may struggle to provide basic necessities.


Social Consequences:



• Social Isolation: Teenage parents may experience social isolation from their peers, as they experience judgement from their community

• Relationship Strain: Teenage pregnancy can strain relationships with family and friends.

• Limited Social Opportunities: Teenage parents may have a limit opportunities to participate in social activities.


Mental and Physical Consequences:


• Mental Health Issues: Teenage mothers are at higher risk for depression, anxiety, and low self-esteem.

• Physical Health Risks: Teenage pregnancies are associated with increased risks of complications during pregnancy and childbirth.


Educational Consequences:

• School Dropout: Teenage pregnancy is a leading cause of school dropout among girls.

• Decreased Academic Performance: Even if they stay in school, teenage mothers may struggle to maintain their academic performance.
